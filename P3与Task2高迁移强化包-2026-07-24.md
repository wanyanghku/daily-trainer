## 二、Part 3 的唯一答题骨架

目标是 **30–45 秒、3–4 句**：

1. **Answer**：直接回答，不绕题。
2. **Reason**：解释最主要的原因。
3. **Example**：给学校、家庭、工作或城市中的简单例子。
4. **Limit/contrast**：补条件或另一面。

### 通用口头骨架

> I think so, mainly because ...  
> This means that ...  
> For example, ...  
> However, it also depends on ...

不必每次四句齐全。说清楚三句，比勉强说六句更符合你的真实语速。

## 三、现有15道Part 3基础题

> 以下保留网站当前答案，只增加中文逻辑提示。目标是理解并能复述，不要求逐字背诵。  
> 第一遍只读“中文链 + 英文答案”；第二遍遮住答案，每题说出3句。

### A. 科技 / AI

核心词：`convenience · face-to-face interaction · over-reliance · independent thinking`

#### 1. What are the differences between online and face-to-face communication?

**中文链**：线上方便快捷 → 面对面能看语气和肢体语言 → 重要谈话更适合当面。

> Well, the biggest difference is that online is much more convenient — you can reach someone instantly, anywhere. But face-to-face feels more personal, because you can read body language and tone, so there's less misunderstanding. For example, a serious talk always goes better in person. That said, for quick daily stuff, online is just easier.

#### 2. Does technology have a negative effect on relationships?

**中文链**：手机使人相处时分心 → 但能维持远距离关系 → 取决于使用方式。

> To some extent, yes. The main issue is people are often on their phones even when they're together — physically there but not really present. You see whole families at dinner staring at screens. But honestly it also helps, like keeping in touch with friends far away, so it really depends on how you use it.

#### 3. Should children be allowed to use AI tools?

**中文链**：允许但要有限制 → AI能辅助解释 → 风险是依赖和停止独立思考 → 家长教师指导。

> I'd say yes, but with limits. AI can be a great learning tool — it explains things simply. But the risk is kids rely on it and stop thinking for themselves. So it's fine as long as parents or teachers guide how they use it.

### B. 教育 / 孩子

核心词：`different ideas · deep focus · short-term motivation · genuine interest · imagination`

#### 4. Is it better to study alone or in a group?

**中文链**：取决于任务 → 小组适合讨论 → 独学适合记忆和专注 → 两者结合。

> It depends on the task, really. Group study is great for discussion and getting different ideas — you learn from others' mistakes. But for memorizing or deep focus, alone works better. Personally I do both — read alone, then discuss with classmates.

#### 5. Do rewards help children learn?

**中文链**：短期有用 → 过度奖励会削弱内在兴趣 → 长期目标是真实兴趣。

> Yes, up to a point. A small reward can motivate kids in the short term. But the danger is they start doing things only for the reward, not out of interest. So rewards are useful early on, but the real goal is genuine interest.

#### 6. How important is imagination for children?

**中文链**：很重要 → 帮助创造性解决问题 → 艺术和科学都需要 → 学校不应只重考试。

> Really important, I'd say. It helps kids solve problems creatively, and it's the basis of art and even science — a lot of scientific ideas start as “what if” questions. So schools should encourage it, not just focus on exams.

### C. 环境

核心词：`daily choices · large-scale change · rewards and fines · lifelong habits`

#### 7. What can individuals do to protect the environment?

**中文链**：个人可回收、坐公交、少用一次性塑料 → 小行动有价值 → 大规模改变仍需政府企业。

> Quite a lot, actually — recycling, using public transport, cutting down on single-use plastic all add up. For example, I try to bring my own bag when I shop. But honestly individual action isn't enough on its own; governments and companies have to do the bigger part.

#### 8. Should governments use rewards or punishments?

**中文链**：两者结合 → 罚款快速制止伤害行为 → 奖励更正面、可能更持久。

> Both, but I'd lean towards rewards. Fines do work — people stop littering if it's costly. But rewards, like subsidies for electric cars, encourage people more positively and last longer. So a mix is probably best.

#### 9. Should schools teach environmental protection?

**中文链**：应该 → 儿童时期形成习惯 → 回收和节水等简单行为可延续到成年。

> Definitely. If kids learn it young, it becomes a lifelong habit — simple lessons about recycling or saving water really shape how they behave as adults.

### D. 城市 / 交通 / 休闲

核心词：`freedom and comfort · traffic and pollution · green space · charging infrastructure`

#### 10. Why do more people own private cars now?

**中文链**：收入上升、汽车更可负担 → 自由舒适 → 代价是拥堵污染 → 需要公交和电动车。

> Mainly because incomes have gone up and cars are more affordable. A car also gives you freedom and comfort, especially for families. The downside is more traffic and pollution — which is exactly why cities are now pushing public transport and electric cars.

#### 11. Are parks important in cities?

**中文链**：重要 → 提供低成本的休息和运动空间 → 对家庭、老人和高密度城市尤其有用。

> Yes, very. In a crowded city, parks give people space to relax, exercise, and get away from the concrete — families and elderly people use them every day. A city with no green space would feel really stressful.

#### 12. What's the future of electric cars?

**中文链**：可能更普及 → 更清洁且有政策支持 → 当前障碍是价格和充电设施。

> I think they'll become the norm — they're cleaner and governments are encouraging them. The main barriers now are price and charging stations, but those are improving fast, so I'm optimistic.

### E. 工作 / 商业 / 社会规则

核心词：`financial security · job satisfaction · fair and safe · customer needs`

#### 13. Is salary the most important thing when choosing a job?

**中文链**：收入对家庭和安全感重要 → 长期还要有工作满意度 → 最好取得平衡。

> For some people yes — especially with a family to support. But personally I think job satisfaction matters more in the long run; if you hate what you do, money won't make you happy. Ideally you want a balance of both.

#### 14. Why should people follow rules?

**中文链**：规则保障公平安全 → 交通规则可防止事故 → 规则合理且目的清楚时更易遵守。

> Because rules keep society fair and safe — if everyone just did what they wanted, there'd be chaos. Traffic rules, for example, prevent accidents. That said, rules should be reasonable; people follow them more willingly when they understand the point.

#### 15. What makes a business successful?

**中文链**：解决真实问题 → 理解客户需求 → 产品体验、团队沟通和努力共同作用。

> A few things — a good product that solves a real problem, and really understanding what customers need. Apple, for example, succeeded by focusing on user experience. Hard work and good communication in the team matter too.

### 15题的最省力复习顺序

1. 先读每题中文链，确认自己能立即选出立场；
2. 每题只记2–4个关键词，不背标点和口头填充词；
3. 遮住答案，用自己的语速说3句；
4. 说不出的题再回看，不重复练已经能清楚回答的题。

## 四、12 个高迁移论证模块

每题最多调用一个模块，不要一次堆多个。

### 1. 便利不等于质量｜Convenience vs quality

**逻辑**：线上工具更方便，但面对面体验、专业判断或实物体验往往质量更高。

> Online services are more convenient and save time. However, they should not completely replace face-to-face contact when trust or careful judgment is important.

可套：线上交流、购物、学习、看展览、远程工作、医疗咨询。

### 2. 可及性与负担能力｜Access and affordability

**逻辑**：好制度首先要让普通人用得起、到得了、看得懂。

> The main benefit is that it gives more people access to the service. However, this will only work if it is affordable and easy to use.

可套：公共交通、教育、医疗、博物馆、科技、文化活动。

### 3. 短期激励与长期兴趣｜Short-term rewards vs long-term motivation

**逻辑**：奖励能让人开始行动，但长期坚持依靠兴趣、意义和习惯。

> Rewards can change behaviour in the short term, but they should not become the only reason for doing something. In the long run, interest and a sense of progress are more important.

可套：孩子学习、工作激励、运动、环保奖励、成绩、抱负。

### 4. 规则必须配合解释和替代方案｜Rules with support

**逻辑**：只处罚容易抵触；解释目的并提供可行替代方案更有效。

> Rules are necessary when individual behaviour affects other people. They are more effective when people understand their purpose and have a practical alternative.

可套：手机禁令、环保法、校规、交通限制、广告监管、宠物管理。

### 5. 个人行动与制度责任｜Individuals and institutions

**逻辑**：个人能改变日常行为，但政府和企业能改变基础设施、价格和行业规则。

> Individuals can make better daily choices, but governments and companies have greater power to create large-scale change.

可套：环境、健康、交通、教育、网络安全、消费。

### 6. 保留传统，但允许适应现代生活｜Tradition and modernisation

**逻辑**：传统保存身份和记忆，但不能完全冻结，适度适应才会继续存在。

> Traditions help people maintain a sense of identity. At the same time, they may need to adapt to modern life so that younger people remain interested.

可套：传统食物、老建筑、博物馆、节日、故事、语言、旅游。

### 7. 经济收益与社会成本｜Economic benefit vs social cost

**逻辑**：商业、旅游和建设创造就业与收入，也可能造成拥挤、污染或过度消费。

> This development can create jobs and income. However, it may also bring social or environmental costs, so sensible management is necessary.

可套：旅游、广告、购物中心、大城市、体育赛事、商业发展。

### 8. 科技应该辅助，而不是替代｜Assist rather than replace

**逻辑**：科技提高效率；核心思考、责任和人际判断仍应由人完成。

> Technology is most useful when it assists people rather than replaces their judgment. Users still need to check information and think independently.

可套：AI、App、教育、医疗、工作、博物馆、沟通。

### 9. 个人自由与公共利益｜Freedom vs public interest

**逻辑**：私人选择应被尊重，但影响安全、儿童或公共环境时需要限制。

> Personal choice should normally be respected. However, some limits are reasonable when an action creates clear harm for other people.

可套：手机、广告、交通、宠物、噪音、环境法、儿童上网。

### 10. 预防通常优于事后补救｜Prevention vs remedy

**逻辑**：处罚或治疗有必要，但教育、基础设施和早期干预更能解决根源。

> Punishment or treatment may be necessary, but prevention is usually more effective in the long term because it addresses the cause of the problem.

可套：犯罪、健康、污染、交通、学习困难、网络依赖。

### 11. 不同人群有不同需求｜Differences between groups

**逻辑**：年龄、收入、居住地和人生阶段会改变选择，避免绝对化。

> It depends on people's age, income and where they live. Younger people may value flexibility, whereas older people often care more about stability and convenience.

可套：科技、工作、住房、购物、休闲、交通、音乐。

### 12. 计划提供方向，灵活性应对变化｜Planning and flexibility

**逻辑**：计划减少不确定性，但过度计划会让人无法应对新情况。

> A plan gives people direction and helps them use time efficiently. However, they should still be willing to adjust it when circumstances change.

可套：日常规律、职业、旅行、学习、团队、重大决定。

## 五、当前新增“近期改变”题组

同桌网页现场核到的问题：

1. Do you think it is good to change one's daily routine?
2. Do you think it is good to change jobs?
3. Is it good for people to get a job promotion?
4. Do people often make plans around their regular routines?
5. Who tends to change their daily routine more, young people or old people?

### 一张卡覆盖五题

> Change can be positive when it improves people's health, efficiency or career development. For example, a new routine may help someone study more regularly, while a new job can provide better experience. However, frequent or sudden change may create stress, so people need a clear reason and a practical plan. Younger people are usually more willing to change because they have fewer fixed responsibilities, whereas older people may value stability.

调用方式：

- routine：用 `health / efficiency + regular study`；
- change jobs：用 `experience / career development + financial risk`；
- promotion：用 `motivation / income + greater responsibility`；
- plans and routines：用“计划 + 灵活性”模块；
- young vs old：用“不同人群”模块。

## 六、现有八个话题池之外的补缺卡

### 1. 医疗与医学研究

**观点链**：医疗工作知识要求高、责任大；公共资金应兼顾基础服务和长期研究；预防降低长期成本。

> Medical work is demanding because mistakes can directly affect people's health. Governments should fund both basic healthcare and research, since one meets current needs while the other may improve future treatment.

### 2. 艺术、博物馆、历史与建筑

**观点链**：实地体验更直观；低价或免费提高可及性；门票可用于维护；旧建筑保存城市身份。

> Museums make history easier to understand because people can see real objects rather than only read about them. Free entry improves access, although a reasonable fee may be necessary for maintenance.

### 3. 商业、消费、广告与金钱

**观点链**：成功企业解决真实问题并赢得信任；广告提供信息也刺激非必要消费；储蓄增加安全感；企业有社会责任但不能代替政府。

> A successful business usually solves a real problem and understands its customers. Advertising can inform consumers, but it may also encourage unnecessary spending, especially when it targets children.

### 4. 信息回复与沟通礼仪

**观点链**：消息适合简单事务；电话或面谈适合紧急、复杂、情绪化内容；延迟回复可能源于忙碌，不应立刻理解为不尊重。

> Messages are convenient for simple information, whereas a call is better for urgent or complicated matters. A slow reply does not always show disrespect, because the other person may be busy or need time to give a careful answer.

### 5. 食物、家庭聚餐与特殊场合

**观点链**：特殊食物保存家庭记忆和文化；家中吃饭利于交流；外出吃饭更方便但更贵。

> Special food is important because it connects a celebration with family memory and cultural identity. Eating together also gives family members a chance to communicate without the pressure of work or study.

### 6. 语言学习与海外工作

**观点链**：语言创造学习和工作机会；科技可辅助但不能取代真实交流；海外工作提升独立性和跨文化沟通，也会带来适应压力。

> Technology can support language learning, but real communication is still necessary for fluency. Working abroad can improve independence and cultural awareness, although adapting to a new workplace may be difficult at first.

### 7. 动物、宠物与自然教育

**观点链**：宠物提供陪伴并教会责任；城市饲养要考虑空间和公共卫生；儿童既要学知识，也要有安全的自然体验。

> Keeping a pet can teach children responsibility and provide emotional support. However, owners must have enough space and follow rules that protect both the animal and the public.

## 七、Task 2 只补两个结构，不背新范文

你已有 Opinion、Discussion 和 Problem–Solution 范文基础。考前只需补齐另外两种题型的任务意识。

### 1. Advantages and disadvantages / Outweigh

**结构**

`Introduction（明确哪边更重要） → 较弱一方 → 较强一方 → Conclusion`

**开头**

> Although this development has some disadvantages, I believe its advantages are greater overall because ...

**主体段**

> One possible drawback is that ... This may lead to ...  
> However, the more significant benefit is that ... For example, ... As a result, ...

如果题目问 `outweigh`，开头和结尾必须明确哪边更重。

### 2. Two-part questions

**结构**

`Introduction → 回答问题 1 → 回答问题 2 → Conclusion`

**开头**

> This essay will explain why ... and suggest that ...

两个主体段各回答一个问号。不要为了使用已背范文而把两个问题写成同一个问题。

## 八、把 Part 3 口头答案扩成作文主体段

### Part 3 版本

> Museums are important because they make history easier to understand. For example, real objects are often more memorable than textbook descriptions. However, entry should remain affordable.

### Task 2 主体段版本

不要增加生僻词，也不要编复杂统计数字。

## 十、考前 70 分钟执行法

### 25 分钟｜15道基础题首次输入

依次读第三节的中文链和英文答案。每题只圈2–4个关键词；能理解即可，不停下来逐句默写。

### 20 分钟｜15道基础题闭卷输出

遮住答案，15题各说30–45秒。说不满没关系，但必须包含“回答 + 原因”；能补例子或条件更好。只标记真正卡住的题。

### 15 分钟｜迁移抽问

快速看第四节12个模块，然后用上面的提示词抽问6题。每题只调用一个模块；答完不重录三遍，只改一个最明显的问题。

### 10 分钟｜Task 2 审题

随机看 5 道题，只写：

- 题型；
- 我的立场；
- 主体段 1 主题句；
- 主体段 2 主题句。

不写完整作文。

最后只检查三个个人高频问题：有没有先直接回答；原因和例子是否真正支持观点；是否因为想说复杂句而中途失控。
